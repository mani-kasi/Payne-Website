About Us

Welcome to Payne

Payne Athletics & Leadership stresses the importance of allowing young individuals to participate in a positive and nurturing environment, learn from each others' experiences, develop friendships and strong leadership traits, inhabit elements of holistic learning and meet individual goals and targets.

Payne prides its vision serving the needs of our athletes, meeting them where they are - on their personal, academic and athletic journey. What started as a single program has now grown into four stand alone programs; Payne includes Payne Basketball, Payne Leadership, Payne Training Club and The Hill Club. Each serving its unique purpose towards athlete’s individual goals and how to achieve it through hard work, hollistics perspective and self care rituals. Incredibly over the past 16+ years Payne’s programs has received over 60 athletes through its services and continues to maintain rapport with most of the past participants in providing guidance, self care resources and references to them. The four program’s success can be seen both on and off the training grounds, as athletes apply skills they learn in their academic and day-to-day lives, through a holistic lens and with the Long Term Athlete Development teaching model.

Payne Training Club

Formerly known as Payne was created in 2006 as a youth based conditioning camp in South Markham/Unionville. The program has served over 40+ students from diverse communities in the Greater Toronto Area. Payne Training Club still serves youth and maturing adults as they prepare during summer in off season conditioning training. Athletes train in small groups in fast paced skill training to lead, collaborate and to build mindset through concepts such as overcoming adversity and hollistic approaches to healthy lifestyle.

Payne L.E.A.D (Leadership, Education, Advocacy and Development)

Payne L.E.A.D. began 2022 at first as a matriculation program meant to work with secondary school student as they embarked on graduating and moving om to Post Secondary. So far Payne L.E.A.D has managed to work with about 10 client on improving their skill sets in matriculation and life organizing.

Payne Basketball

Began in 2006 with initially two high school athletes and grew from there. Payne Basketball since then has worked with over 40+ youth who are dedicated in improving their skill set in basketball.

I have been training with the Payne Training Club for over 3 years now and I have found the program to be very influential to my success as an athlete and a student. As a tennis player competing at the varsity level in Ontario, Payne has helped me substantially raise my level of play, and reach many of my personal goals as an athlete. Training sessions are carefully engineered towards individuals to help them improve their fitness and confidence, while also being cognisant of injuries, and taking all the necessary steps to prevent them from occurring. Training sessions are rigorous and challenging, but I find that the hardships endured at Payne stimulate growth in your physical performance, as well as your approach to everyday life. The difficult training program makes you more resilient, and more adaptable by pushing you to your physical limit, which transfers to life scenarios you would see in school, work, relationships, etc. Additionally, the program nurtures a healthy competitive mindset that pushes individuals to find ways to achieve their goals. The culture/environment of the program is very welcoming and tight-knit. No matter what your background is, or what experience you have, you will be valued and supported throughout the program, while also having an enjoyable time with the people around you. All Payne facilitators have your best interests in mind and are genuinely very compassionate and want to help you succeed in achieving your goals and aspirations as long as you are willing to put in the work yourself as well. Overall, working with the Payne Training Club has been an amazing experience, and I thank Payne for helping me grow into the person that I am today.

My name is Austin Nzige-Nyambok, and I am a 25 year old student-athlete at Algoma University. I have been a part of Payne Training Club, Payne Basketball, and The Hill Club since 2015 and I must say that it has completely transformed my basketball game and overall athletic ability. Payne Training Club is centred around speed, agility, cardio, and functional strength. Through participation in this program I have seen significant improvement in these areas as a result of the various: parachute drills, cone drills, long distance and short distance running drills, band work, and weight training. Payne Basketball is centred around basketball-specific cardio, skill work, plyometric/explosive work. Payne Basketball places a continued emphasis on the ability to shoot while fatigued, as well as make free throws regardless of fatigue-levels or atmosphere intensity. Coach Issah does an incredible job of creating a real basketball game environment filled with a constant fast-pace and potential distractions (ie. noise) in an effort to make these workouts more difficult than a real game so I am able to find a level of comfort when playing collegiate games. Thanks to this training, my: jumpshot, finishing abilities, free throw percentages, defensive capabilities, rebounding, and court vision have seen significant increases. The Hill Club is centred around the improvement of explosiveness and athletic power as we perform various drills at the hill behind Milliken Mills Community Centre. These sessions have helped improved my vertical ability, helping add increases to my vertical as well as my broad jump. As Coach Issah has always reminded us about the potential that hard work has to supersede talent, I implore any individual looking to make significant improvement to their athletic and basketball-specific abilities to enrol themselves in one or multiple of these programs as the results are incredible.
